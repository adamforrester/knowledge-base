Source: https://webaim.org/projects/screenreadersurvey10/
Title: Screen Reader User Survey #10 Results
Author: WebAIM (with research support from BrowserStack)
Survey period: December 2023 – January 2024
Sample: 1,539 valid responses
Captured: 2026-06-18 via firecrawl_scrape

Survey #10 is the latest in the WebAIM screen-reader survey series (since Oct 2009). Survey #11 not yet announced as of capture date.

Primary desktop/laptop screen reader (% respondents):
- JAWS — 40.5%
- NVDA — 37.7%
- VoiceOver — 9.7%
- Dolphin SuperNova — 3.7%
- ZoomText/Fusion — 2.7%
- Orca — 2.4%
- Narrator — 0.7%
- Other — 2.7%

Screen readers commonly used (multi-select; % of respondents using at all, not exclusively):
- NVDA — 65.6%
- JAWS — 60.5%
- VoiceOver — 43.9%
- Narrator — 37.3%
- Orca — 8.3%
- ZoomText/Fusion — 7.5%
- Dolphin SuperNova — 5.4%
- ChromeVox — 3.8%
- System Access / SA to Go — 1.8%
- Window-Eyes — 1.2%
- Other — 5.1%

Operating system: Windows 86.1% / Mac 9.6% / Linux 2.9% / Other 1.4%.

Browsers (primary, when paired with primary screen reader):
- Chrome — 52.3%
- Microsoft Edge — 19.3%
- Firefox — 16.0%
- Safari — 8.0%
- Internet Explorer — 0.9%

Most common screen reader + browser combinations:
- JAWS with Chrome — 24.7%
- NVDA with Chrome — 21.3%
- JAWS with Edge — 11.4%
- NVDA with Firefox — 10.0%
- VoiceOver with Safari — 7.0%
- NVDA with Edge — 5.0%
- JAWS with Firefox — 2.6%
- VoiceOver with Chrome — 2.0%
- Orca with Firefox — 1.9%

Mobile platform: iOS 70.6%, Android 27.6%.
Mobile screen readers commonly used:
- VoiceOver — 70.6%
- TalkBack — 34.7%
- Commentary/Jieshuo — 10.1% (significant in Asia)
- Voice Assistant (Samsung) — 6.0%
- VoiceView — 5.9%

Regional notes:
- North America: JAWS 55.5%, NVDA 24.0% (JAWS dominant)
- Europe: JAWS 29.7%, NVDA 37.2% (NVDA leads)
- Africa/Middle East: JAWS 23.3%, NVDA 69.9% (NVDA strongly dominant)
- Asia: JAWS 22.9%, NVDA 70.8% (NVDA strongly dominant)

The 4-engine × 4-AT matrix per 32 §2.3 is justified by these figures — JAWS+Chrome, JAWS+Edge, NVDA+Chrome, NVDA+Firefox, VoiceOver+Safari are the five combinations that together cover ~75% of respondents. NVDA+Edge is the rounding combination at 5%.
