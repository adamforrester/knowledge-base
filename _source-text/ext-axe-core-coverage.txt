**Critical finding: the canonical Deque figure is 57.38%, not 30–40%.** The 34 §4 reference to "30–40% / 60–70% automated-vs-manual coverage split" cites a *folk-belief* figure Deque has actively worked to disprove since 2021. Inline correction required.

What Deque's 2021 study actually says (verbatim from the coverage report):

> "It is our intention to disprove the widely accepted belief that automated accessibility testing only provides 20 to 30% of accessibility testing coverage."
>
> "This statistic is founded on an inaccurate definition that accessibility coverage is calculated by how many individual WCAG success criteria can be tested by automation."

Deque's methodology and findings:
- 2,000+ audits across various industries; 13,000+ pages/page states; nearly 300,000 issues in the data set
- First-time audits only (each page counted once; only first-audit issues; no remediation bias)
- Automated tests: Deque's axe suite (powered by axe-core); manual tests: Deque's manual testing methodology
- Result: **57.38% of total issues** were identified by automated testing alone

Why the figures differ:
- The "20–30%" / "30–40%" figures (often misattributed to Deque or axe-core) measure *WCAG Success Criteria coverage* — i.e., what percentage of the 50 WCAG 2.1 Level AA criteria can be automatically tested. Deque's analysis confirms this number too: "we found automated issues for 16 out of the 50 Success Criteria under WCAG 2.1 Level AA. This supports the 20 to 30% automated coverage claims that many experts claim today."
- The 57.38% figure measures *issue volume coverage* — i.e., what percentage of total issues found in real audits can be detected automatically. Some criteria (e.g., contrast, name/role/value, parsing) generate many issues per page, and automation catches them at high rates (83.11%, 54.42%, 90.28% respectively).

Top 5 WCAG SC by issue volume in Deque's dataset (Cumulative 78.51% of all issues):
1. 1.4.3 Contrast (minimum) — 88,714 issues; 83.11% automated
2. 4.1.2 Name, Role, Value — 48,287; 54.42% automated
3. 1.3.1 Info and Relationships — 36,382; 45.17% automated
4. 4.1.1 Parsing — 34,488; 90.28% automated
5. 1.1.1 Non-text Content — 23,701; 67.57% automated

100% manual SC examples (automation cannot detect):
- 2.4.3 Focus Order
- 2.4.7 Focus Visible
- 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast
- 1.3.2 Meaningful Sequence

Marcy Sutton's earlier 2016 framing (still on Deque blog at deque.com/blog/accessibility-testing-axe-webdriverjs/, modified Dec 2025): *"Automated testing can free up 30% of your manual testing effort."* This is a *different claim* about manual-effort reduction, not coverage; the "30%" in this older post is often misread as the coverage figure.

axe-core README's exact framing (per search-result snippet): "With axe-core, you can find on average 57% of WCAG issues automatically. Additionally, axe-core will return elements as 'incomplete' where axe-core could not be [certain]."
