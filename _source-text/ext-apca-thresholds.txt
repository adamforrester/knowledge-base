TIER STRUCTURE — Bronze / Silver / Gold

BRONZE LEVEL CONFORMANCE
- Use case: all fluent content text
- No look-up tables
- Exchange Rules: optional
- Body text: Minimum Lc 75 / Preferred Lc 90
- Other content text: Fonts ≥16px / Minimum Lc 60
- Large fluent content: Fonts >32px / Minimum Lc 45 /
  Maximum Lc 90 (large & bold only)

SILVER LEVEL CONFORMANCE
- Use cases: all fluent and sub-fluent content text
- Uses look-up tables or tools for font weight & size
- Exchange Rules: must choose at least one
- Body text & fluent: Per lookup table; minimum font size 14px;
  Maximum Lc 90 for fonts >36px
- Sub-fluent & logos: May reduce contrast by up to Lc 15;
  In no case below Lc 40; minimum font size 10px
- Non-fluent & incidental: May reduce contrast by up to Lc 30;
  In no case below Lc 30

GOLD LEVEL CONFORMANCE
- Use cases: all text (fluent, sub-fluent, non-fluent)
- Exchange Rules: must choose at least two
- Body text & fluent: When used for body text, any contrast value
  less than Lc 75 must be increased by at least Lc 15;
  Minimum font size 16px
- Sub-fluent & logos: May reduce contrast by up to Lc 15;
  In no case below Lc 45; minimum font size 12px
- Non-fluent & incidental: May reduce contrast by up to Lc 20;
  In no case below Lc 30

DIRECTIONAL ASYMMETRY (light-on-dark vs dark-on-light)

The table is used by absolute Lc value — same Lc floor either direction.
The asymmetry is encoded in the equation, not in the floors:

  Normal polarity (dark text / light bg):  S_norm = Y_bg^0.56 − Y_txt^0.57
  Reverse polarity (light text / dark bg): S_rev  = Y_bg^0.65 − Y_txt^0.62

Practitioner rule (quoted): "do not swap the foreground & background colors
in the tool entry fields. The contrast evaluation is based in part on
polarity, i.e. dark-mode or light-mode."

Identical colour pairs swapped will not yield identical |Lc|.

---

WEIGHT AS A SCALING FACTOR

The page does not present a numeric "Lc adjustment per weight step" formula.
Weight scaling is encoded in the table: each weight column gives its own
minimum size for a given Lc, and each Lc row gives its own minimum size
per weight column.

For non-standard fonts, the page describes a manual offset procedure: measure
x-height ratio for size; "Subtract the reference weight from the matching test
font weight. This value is the reference weight offset." Worked example
given: Raleway needs +120 weight offset at 400 and +30 at 700 vs. Arial.

Gold-tier body-text rule that interacts with weight/size: "any contrast value
less than Lc 75 must be increased by at least Lc 15."

---

CONTENT TYPE DEFINITIONS

Fluent — Body Text: "a block or column of more than two continuous lines of
content text that uses a readable font with an x-height of ~19px or less."

Fluent — Primary content not body: "up to two and a half lines of continuous
text" — headlines, captions, primary nav, tooltips, form input.

Large Fluent: "fluent subcategory of 'large content' such as big, bold
headlines, and generally referring to text larger than 36px."

Sub-Fluent: "non-primary content with relaxed readability needs" — secondary
nav, byline, footer matter.

Non-Fluent: "non-content text of an incidental nature" — placeholder, disabled
controls, copyright bugs.

Logo or Branding and Incidental Text in Images also covered.

WHAT THIS PAGE EXPLICITLY DOES NOT COVER

The Myndex/SAPC-APCA index page also points to a Non-Text Contrast guideline,
but that guideline is currently a placeholder ("PLACEHOLDER for the non-text
guidelines (coming soon!)"). The Lc 30 floor for non-text UI components named
in 31 §3 is therefore not yet a published Myndex/ARC value as of mid-2026.
The Lc 30 floor for non-content text in the Silver and Gold tiers above is
the supportable figure; reserve Lc 30 for non-text UI as practitioner
shorthand pending publication.

PRACTICE-FACING IMPLICATIONS FOR 31

31 §3's Lc tier table can now be tightened against this source:
- Lc 90+ "critical UI / super-bold" — corresponds to Bronze "preferred" body
  text and the Silver/Gold ceiling for fonts >36px
- Lc 75–90 "body-text minimum" — corresponds to Bronze body-text minimum,
  Gold body-text floor (rule: < Lc 75 must be increased by ≥ Lc 15)
- Lc 60–75 "large or non-essential text, UI labels" — corresponds to Bronze
  "other content text" Lc 60 floor for ≥16px
- Lc 45–60 "large headings, decorative" — corresponds to Bronze large
  fluent content (Lc 45 floor for fonts >32px)
- Lc <45 — non-text and non-content; the non-text-UI Lc 30 floor is
  practitioner shorthand pending Myndex's published non-text guideline

The line in 31's table about Lc 90+ requiring "14px @ 400 weight" is a
specific over-reading of the matrix: the matrix at Lc 100 in column 400
shows 15.5px (not 14px); the 14px figure in column 400 corresponds to
Lc 80–85, not Lc 90+. Tighten this on the next pass through 31 §3.
