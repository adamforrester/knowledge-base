## Research scope — cover all of the following. Use these as the section spine of the output.

1. **Defining the territory.** The lineages above and how they compete for the same vocabulary. For each: who anchors it, what it optimizes for, what its key vocabulary is, where it has sharpened over time. Close with which framings carry the most weight for a senior leader making decisions in mid-2026 — and which are best treated as correctives.

2. **Personalization vs. adaptation — a critical distinction.** Pull the two apart explicitly because the marketing copy doesn't. Personalization as segment-driven and slow; adaptation as context-driven and fast. The two failure modes (between-session vs. within-session). Where they coexist (e.g. Spotify Home + AI DJ; Google AI Mode on top of personalized results). The diagnostic test: preference and performance moving together (ability-based personalization) vs. apart (system-driven within-session adaptation).

3. **The user experience of adaptive UI.** Trust and the "creepiness" tension — Lumiere/Clippy as the historical anchor and what its actual failure mode was (Swartz's consistency thesis, Reeves & Nass's social-actors framing). The mental-model cost — Findlater & McGrenere 2004, *Ephemeral Adaptation* as the refinement, Butler's *Consistency Is Primitive* as the contrary position. Disclosure, agency, sense of control — visible thinking surfaces, cheap dismissal, side-by-side over in-line. When users want adaptation vs. predictability. The empirical thinness should be named.

4. **What problems does this actually solve.** Hard, evidence-based section. Accessibility (the strongest case, anchored on Gajos/Wobbrock). Discovery and reducing cognitive load for occasional/novice users (Netflix's *Power of a Picture*, Spotify Daily Mix, NN/g on chat-embedded form widgets). Onboarding and time-to-value (Granola's calendar-title inference, Linear's Triage Intelligence). Where it underperforms — power users, regulated UIs, brand-led marketing, professional tools where consistency is a feature.

5. **Ethics, dark patterns, manipulation.** EU AI Act Article 5(1)(a)–(b), live February 2025, with Recital 29's "effect, not intent" framing — adaptive UI that materially distorts behavior is illegal in the EU now. US state regimes arriving in 2026 (Colorado SB24-205, California SB 942, California AB 2013) and what each actually requires. The single most important empirical finding: LLMs are *more* susceptible to choice-architecture manipulation than humans (Liu & He, decoy effect). Appleton's epistemic critique. What designers can concretely do — system prompt as design artifact, deferential UI, audit choice-architecture exposure, maintain auditable variant log.

6. **Industries and use cases.** Strong fit (e-commerce, media, productivity SaaS, accessibility-specific healthcare surfaces, education, fintech consumer apps with strong escape hatches). Ambiguous fit (search, browsers — Arc's quiet retraction is the cautionary anchor, mobile platform shells — Apple Intelligence is the cautionary case). Weak fit (regulated UIs, brand-controlled marketing surfaces, professional tools with strong muscle memory, anything where shared reference matters).

7. **Examples in production today.** Concrete and cited. Not "Linear has AI features" but specifically what they ship and what users see. v0/AI SDK, Linear AI, Notion AI, Anthropic Artifacts, ChatGPT Canvas, Granola, Arc Max + Dia, Apple Intelligence, Spotify + Netflix as the long-running adaptive UI baseline, Google AI Mode, Perplexity Comet.

8. **Where this might be headed (speculative).** Three plausible trajectories given current evidence, with clear `*[speculative]*` labels. What thoughtful practitioners predict (Clark on agent populations; Saarinen on output-isn't-design as the corrective). What is plausible (slot-based generation as the dominant shape, malleable software finding narrow product-market fit, recommendation and generative converging at the layout level, regulatory enforcement landing within 2–3 years, "AI [thing]" branding category exhausting). What is hype.

9. **Brief design systems thread.** Does a design system survive in this paradigm? Yes. The genuine question is what it constrains. The system prompt becomes a design artifact; component metadata grows; variant logging becomes infrastructure. What "consistency" comes to mean. The implementation work is the companion paper.
